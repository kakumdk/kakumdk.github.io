Abey Kuruvilla

On13th Apr 2024, 2025-09-28T08:08:11+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Abey Kuruvilla is a former Indian cricketer who played as a right-arm fast-medium bowler.

Here's an overview of his career:

  • Early Life: Abey Kuruvilla was born on August 4, 1968, in Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharashtra, India.
  • Domestic Career: Kuruvilla represented Mumbai (formerly Bombay) in Indian domestic cricket. He made his first-class debut in the 1991-92 season and played for Mumbai for several years. He was known for his ability to swing the ball both ways and generate bounce off the pitch.
  • International Career: Kuruvilla made his debut for the Indian cricket team in a One Day International (ODI) against West Indies in 1997. He played 10 ODIs for India, taking 10 wickets at an average of 37.50. His international career was relatively brief and he didn't feature in Test matches for India.
  • Indian Premier League (IPL): After retiring from international and domestic cricket, Kuruvilla was involved in coaching and talent scouting. He served as a talent scout for the Mumbai Indians franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL).

  • Post-Retirement: Following his playing career, Kuruvilla has been actively involved in coaching and administration. He has held various coaching roles with the Mumbai Cricket Association (MCA) and the National Cricket Academy (NCA) in India.
  • Cricket Administration: Kuruvilla has also served in administrative roles in Indian cricket. He was appointed as a member of the national selection committee of the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2021.

Abey Kuruvilla's contributions to Indian cricket, both as a player and as a coach/administrator, have been significant. While his international playing career was relatively short, he made valuable contributions to Mumbai cricket and continues to be involved in nurturing young talent in Indian cricket.
