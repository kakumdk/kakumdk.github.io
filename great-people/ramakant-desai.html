Ramakant Desai

On24th Nov 2023, 2024-12-03T09:30:03+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Ramakant Desai (1939–1998) was a former Indian cricketer who played as a right-arm fast-medium bowler. He was a prominent member of the Indian cricket team during the 1960s.

Here are some key points about Ramakant Desai:

  1. Birth and Early Life: Ramakant Desai was born on June 28, 1939, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India.
  2. Cricketing Career: Desai made his debut for the Indian cricket team in 1959. He was known for his ability to generate pace and swing, making him a key bowler for the Indian side during the 1960s.
  3. Test Debut: He played his first Test match against Pakistan in Bombay in 1960.
  4. Memorable Series: One of the highlights of Desai's career was the 1961-62 series against England. He played a crucial role in India's historic Test series victory against England at home.
  5. County Cricket: Ramakant Desai also played county cricket in England, representing Sussex.
  6. Tour of West Indies: Desai was part of the Indian team that toured the West Indies in 1962. His performances were notable during the series.
  7. Injury and Retirement: Desai's career was unfortunately affected by injuries. He suffered a serious knee injury, which eventually led to his premature retirement from international cricket in 1967.
  8. Post-Retirement: After retiring from active cricket, Desai was involved in coaching and administration. He served as the manager of the Indian cricket team.
  9. Death: Ramakant Desai passed away on August 28, 1998, in Mumbai, India.

Ramakant Desai's contributions to Indian cricket during the 1960s, particularly in the historic series against England, are remembered as important moments in Indian cricket history. His skill as a fast bowler and his impact on the game continue to be acknowledged by cricket enthusiasts.
