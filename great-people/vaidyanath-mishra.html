Vaidyanath Mishra

On11th Mar 2024, 2025-09-26T09:58:46+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Vaidyanath Mishra, better known by his pen name Nagarjun, was a renowned Hindi and Maithili poet, writer and literary critic.

Here are some key points about Nagarjun:

  • Birth: Nagarjun was born on June 30, 1911, in Darbhanga, Bihar, India.
  • Education: He studied at the Darbhanga Raj School and later attended Patna College for his higher education.
  • Literary Career: Nagarjun was a prominent figure in Hindi literature and is often considered one of the stalwarts of Hindi poetry. He also wrote in Maithili, his native language.
  • Pen Name: He adopted the pen name "Nagarjun," inspired by the Buddhist monk and philosopher of the same name.
  • Socialist and Marxist Influences: Nagarjun was deeply influenced by socialist and Marxist ideologies and his poetry often reflected his concern for social justice and equality.
  • Works: Nagarjun's poetry is known for its passionate expression, deep reflection and exploration of various social and philosophical themes. Some of his notable works include "Balchanma," "Badal Ko Ghirte Dekha Hai," and "Yahan Se Dekho."
  • Literary Contributions: Apart from poetry, Nagarjun contributed to literary criticism and essay writing. He played a significant role in the progressive writers' movement in Hindi literature.
  • Awards: Nagarjun received several prestigious awards for his contributions to literature, including the Sahitya Akademi Award and the Padma Bhushan.
  • Death: Nagarjun passed away on November 5, 1998, leaving behind a rich legacy in Hindi literature.

Nagarjun's poetry is known for its deep philosophical insights, social commentary and emotional intensity. He remains a celebrated figure in Indian literature and his works continue to be studied and appreciated for their literary and intellectual merit.
