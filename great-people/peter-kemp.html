Peter Kemp

Peter Kemp (1915–1993) was a British author, journalist and intelligence officer.

Here's an overview of his life and contributions:

  • Early Life and Education: Peter Robert Edwin Kemp was born on April 11, 1915, in India, where his father served in the Indian Civil Service. He was educated at Wellington College and Magdalen College, Oxford.
  • Military Service: During World War II, Kemp served as an intelligence officer in the British Army. He was involved in covert operations behind enemy lines, particularly in Eastern Europe and the Mediterranean.
  • Activities in Spain: Kemp is best known for his involvement in the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939), where he fought on the side of General Francisco Franco's Nationalist forces against the Republican government. He served in the British contingent of the Nationalist Army, known as the Spanish Foreign Legion or the Blue Division.
  • Writing Career: After the war, Kemp pursued a career in journalism and writing. He authored several books, including memoirs of his experiences in the Spanish Civil War, such as "Mine Were of Trouble" (1957) and "The Thorns of Memory" (1957). His works provided valuable insights into the complexities of the conflict and its impact on those who participated in it.

  • Literary Contributions: In addition to his memoirs, Kemp wrote several novels and non-fiction works. He also contributed articles and essays to various publications, including The Spectator and The Sunday Telegraph.
  • Later Years: In his later years, Kemp lived in Spain, where he continued to write and reflect on his experiences. He remained interested in political and military affairs, particularly those related to the rise of communism and the Cold War.
  • Legacy: Peter Kemp's writings on the Spanish Civil War are considered valuable sources for historians and researchers studying the conflict. His firsthand accounts provide unique perspectives on the motivations, challenges and moral dilemmas faced by those who fought in the war.

Peter Kemp's life and career are emblematic of the turbulent times in which he lived. His experiences as a soldier, intelligence officer and writer shed light on significant historical events and continue to inform our understanding of the complexities of war and conflict in the 20th century.
