Hazari Prasad Dwivedi

On6th Jul 2024, 2025-02-19T11:25:38+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Hazari Prasad Dwivedi (1907–1979) was a renowned Hindi writer, literary critic, scholar and essayist.

Here's an overview of his life and contributions:

  • Early Life: Hazari Prasad Dwivedi was born on August 19, 1907, in Bailar, a village in Uttar Pradesh, India. He received his early education in Sanskrit and Hindi literature.
  • Literary Career: Dwivedi made significant contributions to Hindi literature through his prolific writing and scholarship. He was a master of various literary forms, including novels, essays, short stories and literary criticism.
  • Novels: Hazari Prasad Dwivedi's novels are known for their depth of insight, rich characterizations and exploration of philosophical and social themes. His notable works include "Banbhatt Ki Atmakatha'' (The Autobiography of Banbhatt), "Charu Chandra Ki Chandani" (The Moonlight of Charu Chandra) and "Rashmi Rathi" (The Sun's Charioteer).
  • Literary Criticism: Dwivedi was also a prolific literary critic and essayist. He wrote extensively on various aspects of Hindi literature, ancient Indian culture and literary theory. His critical works are highly regarded for their erudition and analytical rigor.

  • Revival of Ancient Texts: Hazari Prasad Dwivedi played a crucial role in reviving interest in ancient Indian texts and literature. He translated and edited several classical Sanskrit works, making them accessible to a wider audience.
  • Academic Contributions: Dwivedi made significant contributions to academic institutions and literary organizations. He served as the president of the Hindi Sahitya Sammelan and the Uttar Pradesh Hindi Sahitya Sammelan, advocating for the promotion and preservation of Hindi language and literature.
  • Recognition: Hazari Prasad Dwivedi received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to Hindi literature, including the Sahitya Akademi Award, the Padma Bhushan and the prestigious Jnanpith Award, which he was posthumously awarded in 1973.

Hazari Prasad Dwivedi's works continue to be studied and appreciated for their literary merit and cultural significance. He is remembered as one of the foremost figures in modern Hindi literature, whose writings have enriched the literary landscape of India.
