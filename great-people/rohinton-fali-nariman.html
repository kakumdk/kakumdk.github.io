Rohinton Fali Nariman

Rohinton Fali Nariman is a prominent Indian jurist who serves as a Judge of the Supreme Court of India.

Here's an overview of his background and career:

  • Early Life: Rohinton Nariman was born on August 13, 1956, into a distinguished legal family. His father, Fali Sam Nariman, is a renowned jurist who has served as the Solicitor General of India and was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, for his contributions to law.
  • Education: Nariman pursued his legal education at the Cathedral and John Connon School in Mumbai. He later studied at the Government Law College, Mumbai, where he earned his Bachelor of Laws degree. He then went on to study at Harvard Law School, where he obtained a Master of Laws degree.
  • Legal Career: Nariman began his legal career as an advocate in 1979. He practiced law at the Bombay High Court and the Supreme Court of India, specializing in constitutional, commercial and arbitration law. He was designated as a Senior Advocate by the Bombay High Court in 1993.
  • Judicial Career: Nariman was appointed as a Judge of the Supreme Court of India on July 7, 2014. He has presided over several significant cases during his tenure on the bench and is known for his scholarly approach, clarity of thought and meticulous analysis of legal issues.

  • Legacy: Rohinton Fali Nariman's contributions to Indian law and jurisprudence have earned him widespread acclaim and recognition. He is considered one of the leading legal minds in the country and continues to make a profound impact on the Indian judiciary through his judgments and legal scholarship.
