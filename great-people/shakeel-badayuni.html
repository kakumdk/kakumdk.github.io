Shakeel Badayuni

On26th Mar 2024, 2025-03-19T07:48:55+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Shakeel Badayuni (August 3, 1916 – April 20, 1970) was a highly acclaimed Urdu poet and lyricist in the Indian film industry. He is best known for his evocative and soulful lyrics that have left a lasting impact on Hindi film music.

Here are some key points about Shakeel Badayuni:

  • Birth and Early Life: Shakeel Badayuni was born in Badayun, Uttar Pradesh, India.
  • Education: He was well-educated and obtained his Bachelor's degree from Aligarh Muslim University (AMU).
  • Literary Prowess: Shakeel Badayuni was not only a successful lyricist but also a noted Urdu poet. His poetry was characterized by its depth, beauty and rich language.
  • Entry into Bollywood: Shakeel Badayuni entered the film industry in the 1940s as a lyricist. His association with music directors like Naushad was particularly successful.
  • Collaboration with Naushad: Shakeel Badayuni had a long and fruitful collaboration with music director Naushad. Together, they created some of the most iconic and memorable songs in Indian cinema.
  • Notable Films: Some of the notable films for which Shakeel Badayuni wrote lyrics include "Mughal-e-Azam," "Mother India," "Chaudhvin Ka Chand," and "Gunga Jumna."
  • Mughal-e-Azam: The songs of "Mughal-e-Azam," such as "Pyar Kiya To Darna Kya" and "Teri Mehfil Mein Qismat," became timeless classics and are still cherished by audiences.
  • Awards: Shakeel Badayuni received several awards for his contribution to Hindi film music, including the Filmfare Award for Best Lyricist.
  • Literary Style: His lyrics often reflected deep emotions, love and the human experience. The classical and poetic nature of his writing set his work apart.
  • Death: Shakeel Badayuni passed away on April 20, 1970, at the age of 53.

Shakeel Badayuni's legacy lives on through his timeless and poetic lyrics, which continue to be appreciated by music enthusiasts. His contributions have left an indelible mark on the golden era of Hindi film music.
