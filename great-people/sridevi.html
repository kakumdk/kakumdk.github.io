Sridevi

On7th May 2024, 2025-09-30T17:22:47+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Sridevi, whose full name was Shree Amma Yanger Ayyapan, was one of India's most beloved and iconic actresses.

Here's an overview of her life and career:

  • Early Life: Sridevi was born on August 13, 1963, in Sivakasi, Tamil Nadu, India. She began acting at a very young age and made her debut as a child artist in the Tamil film "Kandhan Karunai" (1967).
  • Rise to Stardom: Sridevi gained widespread recognition and acclaim for her performances in Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam and Hindi films during the 1970s and 1980s. She quickly established herself as one of the leading actresses in Indian cinema.
  • Versatile Acting Talent: Sridevi was known for her versatility as an actress and her ability to portray a wide range of characters with depth and conviction. She excelled in both dramatic and comedic roles and showcased her talent in various genres, including romance, drama, comedy and thriller.
  • Breakthrough Roles: Some of Sridevi's most memorable performances came in films like "Moondram Pirai" (1982), where she played a woman suffering from retrograde amnesia and its Hindi remake "Sadma" (1983). She also delivered stellar performances in films like "ChaalBaaz" (1989), "Mr. India" (1987) and "Lamhe" (1991), among others.

  • International Recognition: Sridevi's talent and charisma transcended borders and she gained international recognition for her work. She was particularly popular in the Middle East, where she was fondly referred to as the "Empress of Indian Cinema."
  • Awards and Accolades: Sridevi received numerous awards and accolades throughout her career, including several Filmfare Awards and the prestigious Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award, in 2013, in recognition of her contributions to Indian cinema.
  • Return to Acting: After a hiatus from acting, Sridevi made a triumphant return to the big screen with the critically acclaimed film "English Vinglish" (2012), where she played the role of a middle-aged woman who enrolls in an English language course to gain self-respect.
  • Legacy: Sridevi's untimely death on February 24, 2018, at the age of 54, shocked and saddened fans around the world. However, her legacy as one of Indian cinema's most beloved and iconic actresses continues to live on. She is remembered fondly for her remarkable talent, beauty and contributions to the world of cinema.

Sridevi's impact on Indian cinema is immeasurable and her influence on subsequent generations of actors and actresses is profound. She remains an eternal symbol of beauty, grace and talent in the hearts of millions of fans worldwide.
