A G Kripal Singh

On4th Apr 2024, 2025-07-22T10:58:29+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

A. G. Kripal Singh, often referred to as Kripal Singh, was an Indian cricketer who represented the Indian cricket team in Test matches.

Here are some key points about him:

  • Early Life: A. G. Kripal Singh was born on December 1, 1939, in Madras (now Chennai), Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Cricket Career: Kripal Singh was a right-handed middle-order batsman and a useful off-spin bowler. He played domestic cricket for Madras (now Tamil Nadu) and made his Test debut for India against England in 1959.
  • Test Career: Kripal Singh played 14 Test matches for India between 1959 and 1964. He scored 584 runs in Test cricket at an average of 25.39, including two centuries and two fifties. His highest Test score was 100 not out against New Zealand in 1965.
  • Domestic Career: In domestic cricket, Kripal Singh was a prolific run-scorer for Madras. He represented the team in the Ranji Trophy and played a crucial role in their success.
  • All-Rounder: Apart from his batting skills, Kripal Singh was also a handy off-spin bowler. He took 34 wickets in first-class cricket at an average of 31.26.
  • Notable Performances: Kripal Singh's century against New Zealand in 1965 was one of his most memorable performances. He also played crucial innings in domestic cricket for Madras.
  • Retirement: Kripal Singh retired from international cricket in 1964 after playing his last Test match against Australia.
  • Later Life: After retiring from cricket, Kripal Singh remained associated with the game as a coach and administrator. He served as a selector for the Indian cricket team and was involved in various cricket-related activities.
  • Death: A. G. Kripal Singh passed away on December 3, 1987, at the age of 48.

Kripal Singh was known for his elegant batting technique and solid temperament. He made valuable contributions to Indian cricket during his playing days and left a lasting impact on the game.
