Gulzar

On16th Jun 2024, 2025-06-04T13:05:47+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Gulzar, whose real name is Sampooran Singh Kalra, is one of India's most celebrated poets, lyricists and film directors.

Here's an overview of his life and contributions:

  • Early Life: Gulzar was born on August 18, 1934, in Dina, Punjab (now in Pakistan). After the partition of India, his family migrated to India and settled in Delhi. His father, Makhan Singh Kalra, was a government servant in the forestry department.
  • Literary Career: Gulzar began his literary career as a poet, writing in Urdu and Punjabi. He adopted the pen name "Gulzar," which means "gardener" in Urdu. His poetry reflects themes of love, loss, longing and the human condition. Gulzar's writing style is known for its simplicity, depth and emotional resonance.
  • Film Career: Gulzar ventured into the Hindi film industry as a lyricist and scriptwriter in the 1960s. He collaborated with music composers like R.D. Burman, Vishal Bhardwaj and A.R. Rahman creating timeless songs that became classics. Some of his notable works as a lyricist include "Kabhi Kabhie", "Anand", "Ghar", "Ijaazat" and "Maachis".
  • Directorial Ventures: Gulzar made his directorial debut with the film "Mere Apne" in 1971. He went on to direct several critically acclaimed films, including "Parichay", "Koshish", "Aandhi", "Angoor", "Maachis" and "Mausam". His films often explore complex human relationships and social issues with sensitivity and depth.

  • Recognition and Awards: Gulzar has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to literature and cinema. He has won several National Film Awards, Filmfare Awards and the prestigious Dadasaheb Phalke Award for lifetime achievement in cinema. He is also a recipient of the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award.
  • Multifaceted Talent: Apart from his work in cinema and poetry, Gulzar has excelled as a short story writer, playwright and children's author. He has published several collections of poetry, short stories and essays. His versatility and creative genius have earned him a prominent place in Indian culture and society.
  • Personal Life: Gulzar was married to actress Raakhee, but the couple later separated. He has a daughter, Meghna Gulzar, who is a noted filmmaker in her own right.

Gulzar's contribution to Indian literature and cinema is unparalleled. His evocative poetry, soul-stirring lyrics and insightful filmmaking have left an indelible mark on Indian culture and continue to inspire generations of artists and audiences alike.
