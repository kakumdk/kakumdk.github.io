Cons:

  • Cookware Compatibility: The cooktop requires induction-compatible cookware, which may necessitate an investment in new pots and pans for some users.
  • Touch Sensitivity: The touch controls can be overly sensitive, leading to accidental adjustments if not handled carefully.
  • Noise Level: The internal fan can be somewhat noisy during operation, which may be distracting in quiet environments.
  • Scratch-Prone Surface: The glass surface, while easy to clean, can be susceptible to scratches if not treated with care.
  • Limited Cooking Capacity: Being a single-burner unit, it may not be suitable for preparing large meals or multiple dishes simultaneously.
  • Power Cord Length: The length of the power cord could be an issue if outlet placement is limited, requiring extension cords in some kitchens.

Verdict:

The Pigeon by Stovekraft Cruise 1800 Watt Induction Cooktop is a reliable and efficient cooking solution for modern kitchens. The compact design allows for easy storage and portability, making it suitable for small spaces or even outdoor cooking.

However, potential buyers should be aware of the need for induction-compatible cookware and the sensitivity of the touch controls, which may require some adjustment. Overall, this induction cooktop is an excellent value for those seeking an efficient, space-saving appliance that delivers consistent performance.

Ideal For: Individuals or small families looking for a space-saving, energy-efficient cooking solution that offers fast cooking and easy cleanup. It’s particularly well-suited for people who often prepare quick meals or have limited kitchen space, such as students or those living in apartments.
