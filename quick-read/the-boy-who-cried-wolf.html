The Boy Who Cried Wolf

By Karthik Kumar D K on 27th Jun 2023, 2025-10-05T09:55:42+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D Kon27th Jun 2023, 2025-10-05T09:55:42+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Once upon a time, in a small village nestled among rolling hills, there lived a mischievous young shepherd boy named Peter. Peter's job was to watch over the village's flock of sheep and protect them from any harm. However, Peter had a tendency to get bored easily and seek attention in unusual ways.

One sunny day, while tending to his sheep, Peter decided to play a trick on the villagers. He climbed up a hill and shouted at the top of his lungs, "Wolf! Wolf! A ferocious wolf is attacking the sheep!"

Hearing the boy's cries, the villagers dropped their work and rushed to the field, alarmed and concerned for their livestock. But when they arrived, they found Peter giggling and rolling on the grass, his sheep peacefully grazing without a trace of danger.

The villagers were frustrated and scolded Peter for his prank. They warned him about the seriousness of his actions, as they had wasted their time and energy in responding to a false alarm. They emphasized the importance of honesty and trust.

Undeterred by their reprimand, Peter found this prank amusing and decided to repeat it. On another day, when he grew bored once again, he climbed up the hill and shouted, "Wolf! Wolf! Please come quickly, the wolf is attacking!"

Again, the villagers, concerned for their sheep and eager to help, hurried to the field. And once again, they found Peter laughing uncontrollably, with no wolf in sight. The villagers were angry and disappointed at being deceived for the second time.

Weeks went by and the villagers began to doubt Peter's credibility. They no longer took his cries for help seriously, dismissing them as yet another prank. Peter's mischief had eroded the trust that once existed between him and the villagers.

Then, one fateful day, a real wolf emerged from the forest and made its way towards the sheep. Terrified, Peter called out for help with all his might, "Wolf! Wolf! Please, somebody, help!"

But this time, the villagers ignored him, believing it to be another false alarm. They had become numb to his cries. The wolf attacked the flock, scattering the sheep in all directions.

Peter watched in horror as the wolf took away some of the sheep he was supposed to protect. He realized the consequences of his actions and understood the importance of honesty and trust. He had lost the trust of the villagers through his repeated lies and now, when he genuinely needed help, no one came to his aid.

The moral of the story is clear: Honesty is a virtue we should always uphold. We must think before we speak and consider the consequences of our actions. Lying and deceiving others can lead to a loss of trust, which is difficult to regain. The tale of the boy who cried wolf reminds us of the importance of honesty, credibility and the trust we build through our words and actions.
