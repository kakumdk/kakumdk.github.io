The Wise Old Elephant

By Karthik Kumar D K on 30th Jul 2024, 2025-01-16T08:17:50+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D Kon30th Jul 2024, 2025-01-16T08:17:50+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Once upon a time, in a vast and ancient jungle, there lived a wise old elephant named Bhaskara. Bhaskara was renowned throughout the jungle for her wisdom, kindness and gentle demeanor. Animals from near and far would seek her counsel whenever they faced challenges or dilemmas.

One day, as Bhaskara was grazing peacefully in a sun-dappled clearing, a young monkey named Yodhaa approached her with a furrowed brow and a troubled expression.

"Dear Bhaskara" Yodhaa said, his voice tinged with uncertainty, "I am faced with a difficult decision and I do not know what to do".

Bhaskara turned her wise eyes upon Yodhaa and gestured for him to sit beside her beneath the shade of a towering tree. "Tell me, young one" she said, her voice soft and comforting, "what troubles you?"

Yodhaa took a deep breath and began to recount his tale. He explained how he and his fellow monkeys had discovered a bountiful fruit tree deep within the jungle, filled with the most delicious fruits they had ever tasted. But as word of the tree spread, other animals began to arrive, eager to claim their share of the bounty.

"Now" Yodhaa concluded, "there is discord among the animals, each claiming that the fruits belong to them. I fear that if we cannot find a solution, conflict will arise and the peace of the jungle will be disrupted."

Bhaskara listened thoughtfully to Yodhaa's tale, her wrinkled brow furrowed in deep concentration. After a moment of reflection, she spoke with a calm and reassuring voice.

"My dear Yodhaa" Bhaskara began, "the solution to your problem lies not in conflict, but in cooperation and understanding. The fruits of the jungle are plentiful and there is enough for all to share if we work together".

With Bhaskara's guidance, Yodhaa and his fellow monkeys devised a plan. They invited all the animals of the jungle to gather beneath the fruit tree and share in the bounty it provided. They set aside their differences and embraced the spirit of unity and cooperation, knowing that together, they could achieve far more than they ever could alone.

As the animals feasted beneath the fruit tree, laughter and joy filled the air and a sense of harmony and goodwill spread throughout the jungle. From that day forth, the animals of the jungle lived in peace and harmony, working together to overcome challenges and celebrate their shared bounty.

And whenever they faced difficult decisions or uncertain times, they would seek the counsel of Bhaskara, the wise old elephant whose guidance had brought them together and whose wisdom continued to inspire them to this day.

The moral of the story is that cooperation and understanding are the keys to resolving conflicts and achieving harmony in our communities and our world. The wise old elephant, Bhaskara, teaches us that by embracing unity and working together, we can overcome challenges and create a brighter future for all.
