Bangalore to Mangalore Solo Trip Plan

On2nd Sep 2023, 2024-10-20T12:31:38+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Solo trip from Bangalore to Mangalore can be an exciting journey, as you'll get to explore the pristine beaches, ancient temples, and vibrant culture of this coastal city in Karnataka.

Here's a suggested itinerary for your trip:

Route

Bangalore -> Hassan -> Belur -> Charmady Ghat -> Mangaluru -> Sakleshpura -> Hassan -> Bangalore

Day 1: Bangalore to Mangalore

  • Start your trip early in the morning from Bangalore.
  • The distance between Bangalore and Mangalore is approximately 350 kilometers, and it takes around 6-7 hours by road.
  • Take the route via Hassan, Sakleshpur or Hassan, Belur to reach Mangalore.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive through the Western Ghats and the lush green landscapes of Karnataka.
  • Reach Mangalore by afternoon.
  • Check into your accommodation and take some time to relax and freshen up.
  • In the evening, you can visit the famous Kadri Manjunath Temple, known for its ancient architecture and beautiful surroundings.
  • Explore the vibrant markets and shopping areas in Mangalore, where you can find local handicrafts and spices.
  • Spend the night in Mangalore.

Day 2: Beaches and Cuisine

  • Start your day by visiting the beautiful beaches in Mangalore.
  • Head to Panambur Beach, known for its golden sands and serene atmosphere.
  • Enjoy leisurely walks along the beach and soak in the coastal beauty.
  • If you're interested in water sports, you can indulge in activities like jet skiing or parasailing.
  • In the afternoon, visit the famous Ullal Beach, which offers a peaceful ambiance and scenic views.
  • Explore the nearby St. Mary's Island, a group of pristine islands with unique rock formations.
  • Don't miss out on tasting the delicious Mangalorean cuisine, known for its seafood delicacies like Mangalorean fish curry and neer dosa.
  • In the evening, you can relax at the beach or visit the Mangaladevi Temple for its evening rituals.
  • Spend another night in Mangalore.

Day 3: Temples and Departure

  • Start your day by visiting the other famous temples in Mangalore.
  • Explore the Mangaladevi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi, which is said to be the origin of the name "Mangalore."
  • Visit the Kateel Durgaparameshwari Temple, located on an islet in the Nandini River, known for its religious significance and scenic beauty.
  • If time permits, you can also visit the Kudroli Gokarnath Temple or the Kadri Sri Manjunatha Temple.
  • In the afternoon, you can explore the Mangalore city and visit the popular attractions like the Sultan Battery, Mangalore Central Market, or the Mangaluru Gate.
  • In the evening, start your journey back to Bangalore.
  • Reach Bangalore by late evening and conclude your solo trip to Mangalore with beautiful memories.

Please note that this itinerary provides a general outline for your trip, and you can modify it according to your preferences and the time you have available. Make sure to check the current travel guidelines, road conditions, and weather forecasts before embarking on your journey. Enjoy your solo adventure in Mangalore.
