Bangalore to Mekedatu Gorge Adventure Solo Trip Plan

On24th Mar 2025, 2025-03-28T09:08:06+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Mekedatu, meaning “Goat’s Leap” in Kannada, is a breathtaking gorge where the Cauvery River flows through narrow rock formations. Located about 100 km from Bangalore, it is an excellent destination for a solo adventure, offering stunning landscapes, river views, and an exciting trek. If you’re looking for an escape into nature, this guide will help you plan a seamless journey.

Route from Bangalore to Mekedatu Gorge

The journey from Bangalore to Mekedatu is around 100 km and takes approximately 3 hours by road.

  • Starting Point: Bangalore
  • Route: Bangalore → Kanakapura → Sangama → Mekedatu
  • Travel Modes:
    • By Bike/Car: Best option for flexibility and scenic stops.
    • By Bus: KSRTC and private buses operate from Bangalore to Kanakapura. From Kanakapura, take a local bus or auto to Sangama, then cross the river and take a local transport to Mekedatu.

Itinerary Summary

  • 6:00 a.m.: Start from Bangalore for a pleasant early morning ride.
  • 8:30 a.m.: Reach Sangama, where the Arkavathi River meets the Cauvery.
  • 9:00 a.m.: Cross the river by coracle or wade through shallow water (seasonal).
  • 9:30 a.m.: Take a bus or trek (4 km) to Mekedatu Gorge.
  • 10:00 a.m.: Explore the stunning rock formations and gushing waters of the gorge.
  • 12:00 p.m.: Enjoy a packed lunch or eat at a local eatery near Sangama.
  • 1:30 p.m.: Visit Chunchi Falls, a beautiful waterfall nearby.
  • 3:30 p.m.: Start the return journey to Bangalore.
  • 6:30 p.m.: Reach Bangalore.

What to Expect

  • Thrilling River Crossings: Experience a coracle ride or wade across the river.
  • Breathtaking Views: Witness the force of the Cauvery River carving through rocky terrain.
  • Exciting Trek: Enjoy a short trek from Sangama to Mekedatu.
  • Refreshing Waterfalls: Visit Chunchi Falls for a scenic break.
  • Peaceful Retreat: Perfect for solitude, photography, and nature exploration.

On the Way: Places to Visit

  • Kanakapura: A serene town with lush greenery, ideal for a short break.
  • Sangama: The confluence of the Arkavathi and Cauvery rivers, a great picnic spot.
  • Chunchi Falls: A beautiful cascading waterfall, best visited in the monsoon or post-monsoon season.
  • Galibore Nature Camp: A wildlife retreat for spotting birds and small mammals.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Mekedatu is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the water levels are manageable.

  • Winter (Nov-Feb): Best time for trekking and river crossings.
  • Monsoon (Jun-Sep): Avoid during heavy rains due to strong currents and slippery trails.
  • Summer (Apr-May): Hot but manageable with an early morning start.

Travel Tips for a Smooth Solo Trip

  • Start Early: Avoid crowds and experience the best of nature.
  • Pack Essentials: Carry water, snacks, sunscreen, and a hat.
  • Wear Comfortable Clothing: Opt for light, breathable outfits and good trekking shoes.
  • Carry Cash: Limited ATMs and digital payment options available.
  • Photography: The gorge and waterfalls offer stunning photo opportunities.
  • Be Cautious: The river can be unpredictable, so avoid swimming in deep waters.
  • Respect Nature: Do not litter or disturb the wildlife.

Conclusion

A solo trip to Mekedatu Gorge is a thrilling experience for adventure seekers and nature lovers. From the mesmerizing rock formations to the refreshing river views, this destination provides a perfect break from city life. Whether you’re trekking, exploring waterfalls, or just enjoying solitude, Mekedatu is an unforgettable escape. So, gear up and set off on an exciting solo journey!
