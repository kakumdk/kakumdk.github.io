How to Treat and Prevent Oral Sores

By Jyothi Karthik D |
on 24th Sep 2024

Oral sores, like canker sores and cold sores, can be painful and annoying. Treating these sores well involves using over-the-counter creams to ease pain and help them heal faster. For canker sores, products with benzocaine or hydrogen peroxide can be helpful. Cold sores, caused by the herpes simplex virus, can be treated with antiviral creams or medications from a doctor.

To prevent oral sores, it’s important to keep good oral hygiene. Brushing and flossing regularly can lower the chance of getting sores by preventing infections and irritation. Avoiding spicy or acidic foods can also help prevent canker sores. For cold sores, managing stress and staying away from people who are infected can reduce outbreaks.

In addition to good oral care, eating a healthy diet is important. A balanced diet full of vitamins and minerals supports your immune system, which can help prevent and manage oral sores. If sores don’t get better or get worse, it’s best to see a healthcare provider for proper advice and treatment.

Key Points:

  • Topical Treatments: Use over-the-counter creams for pain relief and healing.
  • Good Oral Hygiene: Brush and floss regularly to prevent infections.
  • Avoid Triggers: Stay away from spicy or acidic foods to avoid canker sores.
  • Manage Stress: Reducing stress can help lessen cold sore outbreaks.
  • Healthy Diet: Eat a balanced diet to support your immune system.
  • Consult a Professional: See a doctor if sores don’t improve or get worse.
