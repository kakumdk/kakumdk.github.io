Ten best hill stations to Travel Solo in North East of India?

On4th Jun 2024, 2025-10-05T09:55:41+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

The North East of India is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural diversity and serene hill stations perfect for solo travelers.

Here are ten of the best hill stations in North East India for solo travel:

  • Shillong, Meghalaya
    • Known as the "Scotland of the East" Shillong offers lush green landscapes, waterfalls and a pleasant climate. Popular spots include Elephant Falls, Shillong Peak and Umiam Lake.
  • Gangtok, Sikkim
    • The capital of Sikkim, Gangtok is famous for its stunning views of the Kanchenjunga, monasteries and vibrant markets. Don't miss the Enchey Monastery, Tsomgo Lake and the Nathula Pass.
  • Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh
    • Tawang is known for its stunning natural beauty, monasteries and the famous Tawang Monastery. The journey to Tawang itself, passing through Sela Pass, is breathtaking.

  • Pelling, Sikkim
    • Pelling offers magnificent views of the Himalayas, particularly the Kanchenjunga. Attractions include the Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins and the Khecheopalri Lake.
  • Cherrapunji, Meghalaya
    • Famous for its living root bridges, waterfalls and being one of the wettest places on Earth, Cherrapunji is a paradise for nature lovers. Visit the Nohkalikai Falls and the Mawsmai Cave.
  • Ziro, Arunachal Pradesh
    • Ziro is known for its picturesque landscapes, rice fields and the Apatani tribe. It's a great place for cultural immersion and nature walks.
  • Kohima, Nagaland
    • Kohima, the capital of Nagaland, offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Visit the Kohima War Cemetery, the State Museum and nearby villages to experience Naga culture.
  • Mokokchung, Nagaland
    • Mokokchung is a cultural hub in Nagaland, home to the Ao Naga tribe. The town is known for its vibrant festivals, traditional practices and scenic beauty.

Google Ad 2

  • Aizawl, Mizoram
    • Aizawl, the capital of Mizoram, is perched on a ridge and offers panoramic views, rich Mizo culture and vibrant markets. Attractions include Durtlang Hills, Solomons Temple and the Mizoram State Museum.
  • Darjeeling, West Bengal
    • Though not strictly in the North East, Darjeeling is often included in itineraries for its stunning views of the Himalayas, tea gardens and colonial charm. Key attractions are the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, Tiger Hill and the Japanese Peace Pagoda.

These hill stations in North East India offer a perfect blend of natural beauty, tranquility and cultural experiences, making them ideal for solo travelers looking for adventure and peace.
