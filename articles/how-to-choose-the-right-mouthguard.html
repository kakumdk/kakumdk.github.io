How to Choose the Right Mouthguard

By Jyothi Karthik D |
on 14th Aug 2024

Selecting the right mouthguard is essential for effective protection and comfort. Start by choosing the appropriate type: stock mouthguards are pre-formed and inexpensive but often lack a precise fit. Boil-and-bite mouthguards can be softened and molded to your teeth for a better fit. Custom-fitted mouthguards, made by a dentist, offer the best protection and comfort but come at a higher cost.

Comfort and fit are critical in ensuring that a mouthguard provides adequate protection without causing discomfort. It should cover all your teeth securely, allowing you to speak and breathe easily. A well-fitting mouthguard should feel snug and stay in place during activities, without causing irritation or feeling bulky.

Durability and ease of care are also important factors. Opt for a mouthguard made from durable materials that can withstand impacts and frequent use. It should be easy to clean and maintain to ensure hygiene. Regularly inspect the mouthguard for signs of wear and replace it as needed to maintain effective protection.

  • Type: Choose between stock, boil-and-bite, or custom-fitted options.
  • Comfort: Ensure it fits well, allowing for easy speech and breathing.
  • Fit: Should cover all teeth securely and stay in place.
  • Durability: Select high-quality materials that resist wear and impact.
  • Ease of Care: Choose a mouthguard that is easy to clean.
  • Replacement: Regularly check for wear and replace as necessary.
