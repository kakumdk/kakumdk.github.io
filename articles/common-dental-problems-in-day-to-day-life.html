Common Dental Problems in Day-to-Day Life

By Jyothi Karthik D |
on 5th Mar 2025
Updated 2025-03-18T13:14:23+05:30

Maintaining good oral hygiene is essential to prevent common dental issues that can cause discomfort and serious health concerns. Here are some of the most frequent dental problems, their symptoms, and general treatments.

1. Tooth Pain

Cause: Tooth pain usually occurs when deep tooth decay reaches the pulp, leading to bacterial infection.

Symptoms:

  • Severe tooth pain
  • Sudden pain, especially at night
  • Pain while chewing or biting
  • No relief even after taking medication

Treatment: Proper diagnosis is essential. In most cases, a root canal treatment followed by a prosthesis (such as a crown) is required to save the tooth.

2. Tooth Decay (Cavities)

Cause: Tooth decay occurs when food particles stick to the teeth, leading to bacterial infection. If untreated, it can lead to severe tooth pain.

Symptoms:

  • Tooth sensitivity
  • Slight pain while biting
  • Food getting stuck between teeth, making it difficult to clean

Treatment: A restorative treatment such as dental fillings is needed. Early diagnosis can help prevent further complications.

3. Gum Disease (Gingivitis & Periodontitis)

Cause: A bacterial infection causes inflammation of the gums, leading to redness, swelling, and bleeding.

Symptoms:

  • Red or swollen gums
  • Bleeding gums while brushing or flossing
  • Bad breath
  • Tooth sensitivity
  • Gum recession (gums pulling away from teeth)

Treatment: A deep cleaning procedure called ultrasonic scaling is required to remove plaque and bacteria.

4. Tooth Sensitivity

Cause: Tooth sensitivity happens when the protective layer of the tooth (enamel) is worn down, exposing dentinal tubules that react to cold, hot, or sour foods.

Symptoms:

  • Discomfort or pain when consuming cold, hot, or sour foods and drinks

Treatment: Using a desensitizing toothpaste can help reduce discomfort. In severe cases, a fluoride treatment or dental bonding may be required.

5. Bad Breath (Halitosis)

Cause: Bad breath can be caused by plaque buildup, food deposits, poor oral hygiene, dry mouth, or certain foods.

Symptoms:

  • Persistent bad breath
  • Unpleasant taste in the mouth
  • White or yellow coating on the tongue

Treatment:

  • Professional dental cleaning (oral prophylaxis and scaling)
  • Brushing twice a day using the correct technique
  • Using mouthwash and flossing to remove food particles

6. Mouth Ulcers

Cause: Mouth ulcers can be caused by stress, injury, vitamin deficiencies, or certain foods.

Symptoms:

  • Painful sores inside the mouth
  • Discomfort while eating or speaking

Treatment:

  • Topical oral gels to relieve pain and promote healing
  • Avoiding spicy or acidic foods to prevent irritation

Preventive Dental Care

Visiting your dentist every six months for a general check-up can help prevent and detect dental problems early. Good oral hygiene, including regular brushing, flossing, and a balanced diet, is key to maintaining healthy teeth and gums.

By taking proper care of your oral health, you can avoid these common dental problems and ensure a pain-free smile!
