Bangalore to Kukke Subramanya Trip Plan

On18th May 2023, 2025-04-02T08:44:28+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Planning a trip from Bangalore to Kukke Subramanya? That's a great choice! One day trip or Two day trip from Bangalore to Kukke Subramanya, If you are Solo riding on a MotorBike or You are a Family driving in a Car, then this two to three days plan works very well to cover places in Kukke Subramanya.

Kukke Subramanya is a popular pilgrimage destination in Karnataka known for its ancient temple dedicated to Lord Subramanya. Here's a suggested trip plan for your journey:

Bangalore to Kukke Subramanya

  • Start your journey from Bangalore early in the morning to avoid traffic.
  • Take the Bengaluru-Mangaluru Highway (NH75) and enjoy the scenic drive through the Western Ghats.
  • Continue your journey and reach Kukke Subramanya by late afternoon.
  • Check into your accommodation and relax for a while.
  • In the evening, visit the Kukke Subramanya Temple and seek blessings from Lord Subramanya.
  • You can also explore the beautiful surroundings and enjoy the serene atmosphere.

Kukke Subramanya Sightseeing

  • Start your day with an early visit to the Kukke Subramanya Temple for the special morning rituals and Pooja.
  • Afterward, you can have breakfast at one of the local restaurants in the town.
  • Visit the Adi Subrahmanya Temple, which is located near to the main temple. It's a scenic spot surrounded by lush greenery and a small river.
  • In the afternoon, you can enjoy a leisurely walk or hike in the nearby Kumar Parvatha hills. The trek to Kumar Parvatha is a popular activity among adventure enthusiasts and would need one day more for this one.
  • Alternatively, you can visit the Biladwara Caves, which are believed to be the place where Lord Subramanya killed the demon king Tarakasura.
  • Or, spend the evening exploring the local markets, trying out local delicacies and interacting with the friendly locals.

Return to Bangalore

  • After breakfast, check out from your accommodation.
  • Begin your journey back to Bangalore, but you can take a different route this time to explore more places.
  • One option is to take the Sakleshpur-Mangalore Highway (SH88) and visit the picturesque town of Sakleshpur, known for its coffee plantations and scenic beauty.
  • Another option is to take the Charmadi Ghat route and visit Dharmasthala, a famous temple town dedicated to Lord Manjunatha.
  • You can have lunch at a restaurant in either Sakleshpur or Dharmasthala, depending on your chosen route.
  • Resume your journey to Bangalore and reach your destination by evening.

This itinerary provides a general idea of how you can plan your trip from Bangalore to Kukke Subramanya. Feel free to make adjustments according to your preferences, the time you have and any specific attractions or activities you would like to include.
