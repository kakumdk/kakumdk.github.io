World Wildlife Day

On3rd Mar 2021, 2025-06-06T19:42:44+05:30 ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

On 20 December 2013, United Nations General Assembly (UNGA), in its resolution UN 68/205, decided to proclaim 3 March, the international day of the adoption of the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) on the planet raise awareness and benefits fauna and flora in 1973, as World Wildlife Day, which was proposed by Thailand, to celebrate and raise awareness of the world's wild fauna and flora.

UNGA Resolution

  • General Assembly reaffirmed the intrinsic value of wildlife and its various contributions, including ecological, genetic, social, economic, scientific, educational, cultural, recreational and aesthetic, to sustainable development and human well-being.
  • Designating 3 March as World Wildlife Day, in order to celebrate and raise awareness of the world's wild fauna and flora.
  • Also to ensure that international trade does not threaten the survival of species.
